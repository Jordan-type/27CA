Primary Sources of Government Revenue

  • Taxes: Income tax, corporate tax, sales tax, VAT, etc. (Total: KES 100 Billion)
  • Bills and Bonds: Government-issued securities (Total: KES 50 Billion)
  • Debts: Loans from international institutions (Total: KES 80 Billion)
  • Grants: Financial aid from foreign entities (Total: KES 40 Billion)
  • Fees and Fines: Revenue from services and penalties (Total: KES 20 Billion)
  • Natural Resources: Income from natural resource extraction (Total: KES 70 Billion)
  • Privatization: Revenue from the sale of government assets (Total: KES 30 Billion)
  • Remittances: Money sent home by Kenyans abroad (Total: KES 60 Billion)

Prioritization of Funding Sources

Based on strategic goals, revenue from these sources should be allocated as follows:

  • Infrastructure Development: Prioritize funding from Taxes and Bills/Bonds.
  • Healthcare: Use Grants and Natural Resource revenue.
  • Education: Allocate funds from Taxes and Fees/Fines.
  • Social Services: Direct revenue from Debts and Grants.
  • Defense: Utilize revenue from Privatization and Natural Resources.
